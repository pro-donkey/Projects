Classic Vada Pav Recipe



    Ingredients:
  • For the Potato Vada:
  • 4 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • A few curry leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Oil, for deep frying
  • For the Pav (Buns):
  • 4 pav buns or burger buns
  • Butter, for toasting
  • For Garnish:
  • Green chutney (made with mint and coriander)
  • Tamarind chutney
  • Thinly sliced onions
  • Chopped fresh coriander leaves
    Instructions:
  • 1. Prepare the Potato Vada:
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and asafoetida (hing). Let them splutter.
  • Stir in the chopped green chilies, grated ginger, and minced garlic. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  • Add the chopped curry leaves, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Mix well.
  • Add the mashed potatoes and salt. Stir to combine all the ingredients thoroughly. Cook for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potato mixture is heated through and well combined.
  • Remove the potato mixture from heat and let it cool slightly.
  • Divide the potato mixture into equal-sized balls and shape them into patties or vadas.
  • 2. Deep Fry the Potato Vadas:
  • Heat oil for deep frying in a deep pan or kadai over medium-high heat.
  • Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in the potato vadas, a few at a time.
  • Fry the vadas until they turn golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. This usually takes about 5-6 minutes.
  • Using a slotted spoon, remove the fried potato vadas from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  • 3. Prepare the Pav:
  • Heat a griddle or tava over medium heat. Slice the pav buns in half horizontally.
  • Spread a little butter on the cut sides of the pav buns and toast them on the griddle until they are lightly browned and crisp.
  • 4. Assemble the Vada Pav:
  • Spread green chutney on one side of the pav bun and tamarind chutney on the other side.
  • Place a hot potato vada in the center of the bun.
  • Garnish with thinly sliced onions and chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  • 5. Serve:
  • Serve your classic Vada Pav immediately with extra chutney on the side and enjoy this delicious street food snack!
