Diseases of raspberries



    raspberries Mosaic Virus
  • Purple black cankers form at wounds on young canes; cankers enlarge to encircle cane and cause wilting and death of lateral shoots; infected canes are often cracked and brittle, breaking easily; black specks (fungal fruiting bodies) may become visible in the cankers.
  • Spread by rain splash from infected to healthy canes and Fungus.
  • Always plant raspberries in full sun and in an area with good drainage; plant only certified planting material
  • avoid over fertilizing plants
  • remove and destroy fruiting canes immediately after harvest , if pruning is necessary then make cuts during dry weather to allow wounds to heal before wet weather
