Classic Momos Recipe



    Ingredients:
  • For the Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed
  • For the Filling:
  • 1/2 pound (225 grams) ground chicken, pork, or vegetables (cabbage, carrots, and mushrooms work well)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • For the Dipping Sauce (Optional):
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • For Steaming:
  • Oil or cooking spray, for greasing
    Instructions:
  • 1. Prepare the Dough:
  • In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt.
  • Add water gradually while kneading until you have a smooth, elastic dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  • 2. Prepare the Filling:
  • In a skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  • Add the finely chopped onion, minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© for a few minutes until the onions turn translucent.
  • Add the ground meat or vegetable mixture and cook until it's no longer pink (for meat) or until the vegetables are tender.
  • Stir in the soy sauce, salt, and black pepper. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the mixture is well combined and any excess moisture has evaporated.
  • Remove the filling from the heat and let it cool completely.
  • 3. Shape the Momos:
  • Divide the rested dough into small, equal-sized balls.
  • Roll each ball into a thin, round wrapper, about 3-4 inches in diameter. You can use a rolling pin or your hands to achieve this.
  • Place a spoonful of the cooled filling in the center of each wrapper.
  • Fold the wrapper in half, enclosing the filling, and then pinch the edges together to seal it. You can create pleats along the edges to give them a decorative look.
  • 4. Steam the Momos:
  • Grease a steamer basket or tray with oil or cooking spray to prevent sticking.
  • Arrange the momos in the steamer, leaving some space between them to prevent sticking.
  • Steam the momos for about 15-20 minutes until they become translucent and the dough is cooked through.
  • 5. Prepare the Dipping Sauce (Optional):
  • In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and sugar to make a dipping sauce.
  • 6. Serve:
  • Transfer the steamed momos to a serving plate.
  • Serve your classic momos hot with the optional dipping sauce, and enjoy this delicious Nepali and Tibetan dumpling dish!
