Classic Vegetable Samosa Recipe



    Ingredients:
  • For the Samosa Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed
  • For the Samosa Filling:
  • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup boiled and chopped mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, and green beans)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • For Frying:
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying
    Instructions:
  • 1. Prepare the Samosa Dough:
  • In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and 1/4 cup of vegetable oil or ghee. Mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  • Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead the dough until it's smooth and firm. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  • 2. Prepare the Samosa Filling:
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add the chopped onions, minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  • Stir in the garam masala, turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are fragrant.
  • Add the boiled and mashed potatoes and chopped mixed vegetables. Mix well, ensuring the spices coat the vegetables evenly. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the filling is heated through and well combined.
  • Remove the filling from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  • 3. Shape and Fill the Samosas:
  • Divide the rested dough into equal-sized balls, about the size of a golf ball.
  • Roll each dough ball into a thin oval or circle, about 6 inches in diameter.
  • Cut the rolled dough in half to create two semi-circles.
  • Take one semi-circle, fold it in half to create a cone shape, and seal the edge by moistening it with a little water.
  • Fill the cone with the cooled potato and vegetable mixture.
  • Seal the open edge of the cone to form a triangular samosa. Press the edges firmly to ensure they are sealed well.
  • Repeat this process with the remaining dough and filling.
  • 4. Deep Fry the Samosas:
  • Heat vegetable oil for deep frying in a deep pan or kadai over medium-high heat.
  • Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in the prepared samosas, a few at a time.
  • Fry the samosas until they turn golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. This usually takes about 8-10 minutes.
  • Using a slotted spoon, remove the fried samosas from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  • 5. Serve:
  • Let the samosas cool for a few minutes before serving.
  • Enjoy your classic Vegetable Samosas with tamarind chutney, mint chutney, or your favorite dipping sauce!
