Diseases of Grapes



    Grapes Black Rot
  • Black rot, caused by the fungus Guignardia bidwellii, is a serious disease of cultivated and wild grapes. The disease is most destructive in warm, wet seasons.     Grapes leaf blight
  • Northern grapes leaf blight is favoured by wet humid cool weather typically found later in the growing season. Spores of the fungus that causes this disease can be transported by wind long distances from infected fields. Spread within and between fields locally also relies on wind blown spores.
