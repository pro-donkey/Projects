Classic Kadai Paneer Recipe



    Ingredients:
  • 250 grams paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), thinly sliced
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • For the Kadai Masala:
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon dried red chili flakes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 whole black peppercorns
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
    Instructions:
  • 1. Prepare the Kadai Masala:
  • Heat a dry pan over medium heat and add all the kadai masala ingredients - coriander seeds, dried red chili flakes, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, green cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick.
  • Roast the spices for 2-3 minutes until they become fragrant. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  • Remove the roasted spices from the pan and let them cool slightly.
  • Grind the roasted spices into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Set aside.
  • 2. Prepare the Kadai Paneer:
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a kadai or large skillet over medium heat.
  • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns golden brown.
  • Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the raw aroma disappears.
  • Add the tomato puree and cook for 5-6 minutes until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  • Add the ground kadai masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  • Stir in the sliced bell peppers and cook for 2-3 minutes until they start to soften.
  • Add the paneer cubes and gently mix them with the masala, ensuring they are coated evenly.
  • Crush the dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) between your palms and add them to the kadai.
  • Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the paneer to absorb the spices.
  • Sprinkle garam masala over the kadai paneer and give it a final stir.
  • 3. Serve:
  • Garnish your classic Kadai Paneer with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice. Enjoy this flavorful and spicy Indian paneer dish!
