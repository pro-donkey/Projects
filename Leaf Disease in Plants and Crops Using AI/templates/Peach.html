Diseases of Peach



    Peach Mosaic Virus
  • This disease is caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas arboricola pv. pruni, and affects peach fruit and leaves
  • infected leaves develop small reddish-purple spots that often have a white center. In advanced cases, the inner portion of the spot often falls out, giving the leaf a “ragged” or “shot-hole” appearance.
  • This disease is difficult to control, and chemical sprays are not practical for the home gardener. Varieties are available that are moderately resistant but not immune.Bacterial spot is usually more severe on poorly nourished trees or where nematodes are a problem, so proper cultural care is important.
